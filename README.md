# Walmart Retail Analytics, Demand Forecasting, and Business Intelligence Project

## Project Overview

Retail organizations generate large volumes of operational and sales data every day. Converting this information into meaningful business decisions requires a structured analytics process that can identify performance trends, explain business outcomes, and support future planning.

This project presents a complete retail analytics workflow developed using Walmart sales data. The objective was to build a practical analytics solution that not only explains historical performance but also supports future decision-making through forecasting, statistical testing, and business intelligence reporting.

The project integrates Python, SQL, Excel, Power BI, and Tableau into a single analytics pipeline and demonstrates how different tools can work together to solve real business problems.

---

## Business Objectives

The project was designed to answer several key business questions:

* Which stores and departments contribute the most to overall revenue?
* How do holidays and external economic conditions influence sales performance?
* Are there measurable differences in sales patterns across store types?
* Can future demand be forecasted accurately enough to support inventory planning?
* How can business users interact with data through executive dashboards?

---

## Data Sources

The analysis combines multiple datasets from Walmart's retail operations:

* Historical weekly sales data
* Store information
* Economic and environmental indicators
* Holiday information

These datasets were integrated into a unified analytical dataset for further analysis and reporting.

---

## Project Workflow

### 1. Data Preparation

The first stage involved cleaning and transforming raw data.

Key activities included:

* Data validation
* Missing value treatment
* Date transformation
* Feature engineering
* Dataset integration

Additional features such as Year, Month, and Week were created to support trend analysis and forecasting.

---

### 2. Exploratory Data Analysis

The exploratory analysis focused on understanding sales behavior across different dimensions of the business.

Analysis areas included:

* Store-level performance
* Department-level performance
* Sales trends over time
* Holiday effects
* Economic factor analysis

Visualizations were used extensively to identify patterns, seasonality, and potential business opportunities.

---

### 3. SQL Analytics

Business-focused analytics were conducted using both MySQL and PostgreSQL.

The SQL component included:

* Revenue ranking by store
* Department performance analysis
* Running totals and trend calculations
* Common Table Expressions (CTEs)
* Window functions
* Sales anomaly detection
* KPI generation

This stage demonstrated how SQL can be used to support operational and strategic reporting.

---

### 4. Demand Forecasting

A forecasting model was developed using Prophet.

The model was designed to:

* Capture long-term trends
* Detect seasonality
* Forecast future sales demand
* Estimate uncertainty through confidence intervals

The final forecasting output provides projected sales values for future periods and can support inventory planning and resource allocation decisions.

---

### 5. Statistical Testing

To move beyond descriptive analysis, statistical testing was incorporated into the project.

A/B testing was performed to evaluate:

* Holiday versus non-holiday sales performance
* Performance differences across store types

Hypothesis testing was used to determine whether observed differences were statistically significant.

---

### 6. Business Intelligence Dashboards

Interactive dashboards were developed using Power BI and Tableau.

The dashboards provide:

* Executive KPI summaries
* Sales trend analysis
* Store performance monitoring
* Forecast visualizations
* Interactive filtering and exploration

The Tableau dashboard was structured as a business story, guiding users from overall performance analysis to forecasting and business recommendations.

---

## Key Findings

Several important insights emerged from the analysis.

### Revenue Concentration

A relatively small number of stores account for a substantial share of total revenue, highlighting opportunities for targeted investment and performance benchmarking.

### Holiday Impact

Sales patterns change significantly during holiday periods, indicating the importance of inventory and staffing adjustments during peak demand periods.

### External Influences

Variables such as CPI, fuel prices, and unemployment show measurable relationships with sales performance and should be considered when evaluating market conditions.

### Predictable Demand Patterns

Historical sales data demonstrates recurring seasonal behavior, making forecasting a valuable tool for future planning.

---

This project demonstrates how analytics can support practical business decisions.

Potential applications include:

* Inventory planning

The outputs can help retail managers make more informed decisions using evidence rather than intuition.
